Overview

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has revolutionized many industries, and photography is no exception. Over the last few years, AI-powered tools have reshaped how photographers capture, edit, and share their work. From automatic scene detection to enhanced image quality, AI has become a valuable asset in both professional and amateur photography. One of the most significant AI contributions is in the realm of image enhancement. Tools like Adobe Lightroom and Photoshop now feature AI-driven features such as Adobe Sensei, which can automatically adjust settings like exposure, contrast, and color balance based on the image’s content. This means photographers can spend less time manually editing and more time focusing on the creative aspects of their work.

Presets have become an essential tool in the photographer’s workflow, particularly for those looking to save time while maintaining consistency across their images. Whether used for quick edits or as starting points for more detailed adjustments, presets allow photographers to apply a set of predefined adjustments to their photos with a single click. The most common type of preset is the "filter" used in programs like Lightroom, where photographers can apply color grading, exposure adjustments, and other settings to an entire set of photos at once. Presets are popular for streamlining the editing process, especially for photographers working on large volumes of images, such as wedding or event photographers.

As technology continues to evolve, the combination of AI tools and presets is paving the way for a new era in photography. Photographers can now streamline their editing workflow by utilizing AI-powered presets that automatically adjust images based on the content and context of each shot. This innovative approach is transforming how photographers approach editing and image enhancement. AI-driven presets work by analyzing the details of a photo—such as lighting, subject matter, and composition—and then applying the most appropriate preset adjustments. For example, AI may detect that a portrait image requires skin smoothing and eye enhancement or that a landscape shot needs more vibrant colors and sharper details. This level of automation significantly reduces the time and effort required for manual adjustments, allowing photographers to focus more on the artistic side of their craft.
